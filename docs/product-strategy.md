# Product Strategy

## Core Thesis

Ubuntu Sentinel is not a reporting app. It is community intelligence infrastructure that shifts information power, validation power, narrative power, and accountability power toward communities closest to conflict and peacebuilding work.

## Why It Exists

Calls, SMS, WhatsApp, email, hotlines, and local meetings already exist. They are intake channels. The missing layer is what happens after intake:

- one structured case record,
- preserved original testimony,
- consent and safety checks,
- community validation,
- policy/promise comparison,
- reporter status visibility,
- escalation-ready accountability briefs,
- evidence of resilience, not only harm.

## OSF Alignment

Ubuntu Sentinel serves the intersection of:

- Democratic Futures: civic voice, accountability, youth participation.
- Transformative Peace: community-led peace infrastructure and reparative justice.
- Resources Futures: extraction, CDA, land, water, and community benefit promise tracking.

## Differentiators

- USSD-style intake for feature-phone and low-connectivity contexts.
- Dual-zone map: conflict zones and resilience zones.
- Four-agent Codex pipeline visible to judges.
- Seeded policy/RAG comparison for promise-delivery gaps.
- Community validation gate before escalation.
- Role-shaped views for reporter, validator, advocate, and OSF partner.

## Demo Narrative

The demo should make this point quickly:

> OSF's philosophy is not only about documenting Africa's problems. It is about making Africa's existing peace systems visible, actionable, and defensible.

## Phase 2 Narrative

After demo day, the architecture can grow into:

- oral history and peace memory registry,
- women peacebuilder registry,
- satellite imagery correlation,
- production IAM,
- full consent lifecycle,
- real WhatsApp and partner integrations.
